<p style="text-align: justify;">
I am a third-year Ph.D. candidate in psychology advised by Profs. <a href="https://psych.princeton.edu/people/elke-weber">Elke Weber</a> and <a href="https://psychology.princeton.edu/people/natalia-v%C3%A9lez">Natalia Velez</a>. My research investigates the role of social learning in different aspects of judgement and decision-making. In one of my current lines of work, I study social learning vis-á-vis social norms, investigating the role norms play in coordination. In another line of work, I study how social learning facilitates exploration, with a focus on decision-making under uncertainty and concept formation. 
<br><br>
The Hamid Biglari *87 Behavioral Science Fellowship from the Kahneman-Treisman Center supports my research. Before coming to Princeton, I earned a Master's degree in social sciences with a certificate in computational social sciences from the University of Chicago and a Bachelors in psychology from Stephen F. Austin State University.
</p>
